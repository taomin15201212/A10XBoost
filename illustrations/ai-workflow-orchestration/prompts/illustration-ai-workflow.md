# Article Illustration Prompts

## Style Guide: Notion Style

Notion style characteristics:
- Minimalist hand-drawn line art
- Monochrome or limited color palette (black/gray on white/off-white)
- Imperfect, sketchy lines that look human-drawn
- Clean, uncluttered compositions
- Geometric shapes with hand-drawn quality
- Light, airy feel
- Professional but approachable

## Illustration 1: Fragmented Tools vs Orchestrated Workflow

**Filename**: 01-comparison-fragmented-vs-orchestrated.png
**Aspect Ratio**: 16:9

### Prompt

A split-screen comparison diagram in minimalist hand-drawn line art style (Notion style).

LEFT SIDE - "Tool Stacking" (labeled in Chinese "工具拼接"):
- Three disconnected tool icons (labeled A, B, C with simple geometric shapes)
- Dashed arrows between them showing manual copy-paste
- Small human figure frantically copying data from A to B, B to C
- Visual "friction" marks or broken connection symbols
- Chaotic, messy layout

RIGHT SIDE - "Workflow Orchestration" (labeled in Chinese "工作流编排"):
- Central orchestrator node (conductor podium icon)
- Connected to three tool icons in harmonious arrangement
- Solid flowing lines showing seamless data flow
- Same human figure now relaxed, conducting/observing
- Orchestra metaphor: tools as instruments, orchestrator as conductor

Style notes:
- Black lines on off-white background
- Hand-drawn imperfect lines and circles
- Left side slightly chaotic, right side organized
- Chinese labels in clean sans-serif
- Simple geometric tool icons (square, circle, triangle)
- Flowing organic arrows on right, jagged broken lines on left
